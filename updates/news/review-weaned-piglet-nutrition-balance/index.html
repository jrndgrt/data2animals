Review on balancing amino acids, energy, and protein in weaned piglets

Key takeaways

  • The review frames amino acids, protein, and energy as connected diet factors rather than separate single targets.
  • It focuses on practical nutrition choices for weaned piglets, where performance, gut health, and welfare need to be balanced.
  • The paper highlights that reducing crude protein or changing energy supply should be evaluated together with amino acid adequacy.
  • The main message is cautious and applied: diet formulation after weaning benefits from integrated decisions rather than isolated nutrient adjustments.
