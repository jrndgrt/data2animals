New HS-SPME Arrow GC-MS system for digestion-metabolite analysis

LANUPRO is acquiring a new automated HS-SPME Arrow GC-MS platform to strengthen volatile metabolite analysis in feed, digesta, feces, and related animal-nutrition matrices.

LANUPRO is expanding its analytical portfolio with a new automated Headspace-SPME Arrow GC-MS system. The instrument is coming to LANUPRO through a shared acquisition with Prof. Thomas Van Hecke and colleagues, and will be used to strengthen the analysis of digestion-related volatile metabolites in feed, digesta, feces, fermentation models, and food-animal research.

Instrument acquisition

Direct sampling of volatile digestion markers

Headspace-SPME is a solvent-minimising sampling technique for volatile and semi-volatile compounds. Instead of lengthy extraction and clean-up, a coated fibre is exposed to the headspace above a sample. Heating and shaking can help compounds move from the matrix into the headspace, where they are trapped on the coating and then released in the hot GC inlet.

Minimal preparation

Samples can be screened without extensive solvent extraction, reducing preparation time and matrix handling.

SPME Arrow

SPME Arrow is a more robust SPME format with a larger sorptive phase than a classical fibre, improving capacity, sensitivity, and repeatability for automated work.

GC separation

Volatile compounds are separated before detection, which helps resolve complex profiles from fermentation and digestion matrices.

Mass spectrometry

MS detection adds compound identification and targeted quantification; MS/MS workflows can further improve selectivity in complex samples.

The platform is especially relevant for volatile markers that are informative but difficult to handle with conventional workflows. For LANUPRO, the priority is to connect chemical readouts with animal-nutrition expertise: short-chain and branched-chain fatty acids, fermentation-derived volatiles, aroma-active compounds, and other metabolites that help interpret microbial fermentation, gut function, feed quality, and product quality.

The Arrow format should make the workflow more suitable for routine studies because it combines direct headspace sampling with automated injection and controlled extraction conditions. This is important when many samples must be compared across diets, treatments, time points, or sample matrices. The GC-MS readout then gives a chemical fingerprint that can be linked to wet-chemistry results, microbiome data, animal performance, and histological or physiological endpoints.
