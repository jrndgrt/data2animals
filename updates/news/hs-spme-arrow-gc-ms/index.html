New HS-SPME-Arrow-GC-MS system for volatile metabolite analysis

We were granted support to acquire a new automated HS-SPME-Arrow-GC-MS platform for volatile metabolite research in feed, digesta, and feces.

We have been granted support to acquire a new HS-SPME-Arrow-GC-MS system in collaboration with Thomas Van Hecke and colleagues. The installation is a major upgrade for volatile metabolite analysis because it combines automation, tight control of the injection step, and SPME Arrow technology in one platform.

Instrument acquisition

A more controlled workflow for volatile compounds

The new system will replace an older HS-SPME-GC-MS setup and make it possible to work with better reproducibility and throughput. That matters for food, intestinal contents, feces, and other matrices where volatile profiles are an important readout.

AutomationInjection robot SPME ArrowImproved sampling Use casesFood and digesta
Volatile profiling

Measure compounds that are difficult to capture with standard workflows.

Better control

Reduce variation through a tightly controlled and automated injection step.

Broader applications

Support nutrition, gut health, food quality, and digestion research.

Shared use

Create a platform that benefits several projects and partners across the lab.

The instrument will be used for volatile metabolites in feed and digestion research, including short-chain fatty acids, ammonia-related readouts, and other compounds that help interpret fermentation and gut function.
