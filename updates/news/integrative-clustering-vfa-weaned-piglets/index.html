New paper on volatile fatty acid phenotypes in weaned piglets

Key takeaways

  • Clustering colonic volatile fatty acid profiles separated weaned piglets into distinct fermentation phenotypes.
  • The high short-chain fatty acid phenotype was linked with lower ammonia and serum urea, a lower colonic pH, and better growth.
  • The high branched-chain fatty acid phenotype showed signs of more protein fermentation and a less favourable intestinal environment.
  • Specific bacterial groups helped distinguish the phenotypes, showing how fermentation patterns connect microbiota, gut physiology, and performance.
