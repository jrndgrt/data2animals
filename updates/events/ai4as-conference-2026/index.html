Ghent, Belgium

AI4AS 2026: Artificial Intelligence for Animal Science

Ghent hosts AI4AS 2026 with two conference days, an ILVO technical tour, and two summer-school days on computer vision and machine learning.

29 June to 3 July 2026

AI, data, and animal science in one week

AI4AS brings researchers, animal scientists, engineers, data scientists, and livestock-sector partners to Ghent to discuss how artificial intelligence can support more efficient, sustainable, healthy, and welfare-oriented animal production.

Conference 29-30 Jun Technical tour 1 Jul Summer school 2-3 Jul

Conference days

29-30 June

Day 1

Conference opening

The first conference day focuses on data collection, processing, standardisation, and integration. The program brings together sensor data, IoT devices, labels, metadata, privacy, and interoperable data flows, then moves into emerging AI applications in precision livestock farming and digital twinning.

Parallel sessions and posters create space for discussion around farm adoption, AI for science, and datasets and benchmarks. The day closes with networking and the welcome reception.

Day 2

Conference plenary and applied AI

The second conference day opens with the plenary and continues with sessions on datasets and benchmarks, digital biomarkers and phenotypes, and the ethical, environmental, and social implications of AI in animal science.

The focus is on how methods can move from prototype to practice, with an emphasis on robust evaluation, sector uptake, and the research questions that still need better evidence.

Technical tour

1 July

Day 3

ILVO technical tour

The technical tour takes participants to ILVO T&V115 in Merelbeke-Melle. The day runs from 10:00 to 16:00 and starts with bus transport from the Ghent conference venue.

Participants are divided into four subgroups and rotate through short demonstrations led by ILVO staff. The program includes a keynote on large language models, foundation models, and knowledge graphs, followed by demonstrations on AI in livestock farming, digitalisation and data sharing, emissions, renewable energy, and agritech. The tour also gives a broader view of work from the ILVO Technology and Food Science Unit and Animal Science Unit, plus contributions from Ghent University and KU Leuven.

Summer school

2-3 July

Day 4

Computer vision for animal science

The first summer-school day is devoted to computer vision applications in animal science. It takes place in room A1.4 on Campus Coupure.

The emphasis is on how image analysis and visual sensing can support monitoring, counting, tracking, and interpretation of animal behaviour and production processes.

Day 5

Machine learning pitfalls

The second summer-school day shifts from vision to model reliability. It focuses on the pitfalls of machine learning in animal science, again in room A1.4 on Campus Coupure.

The day is intended to help participants think critically about model design, validation, overfitting, data leakage, and interpretation, so that AI tools remain useful in practice.

Conference venue

Campus Tweekerken hosts the conference on 29-30 June. The venue is in central Ghent and is easy to reach from Gent-Sint-Pieters station.

Tour venue

The ILVO visit takes place at T&V115, Burg. van Gansberghelaan 115 bus 1, 9820 Merelbeke-Melle.

Summer-school venue

The workshop days take place at Campus Coupure, Building A, room A1.4.
