Ghent, Belgium

AI4AS 2026: Artificial Intelligence for Animal Science

Ghent hosts the second AI4AS conference, with a two-day scientific program, an ILVO technical tour, and two focused summer-school workshops.

29 June to 3 July 2026

AI, data, and animal science in one week

AI4AS brings researchers, animal scientists, engineers, data scientists, and livestock-sector partners to Ghent to discuss how artificial intelligence can support more efficient, sustainable, healthy, and welfare-oriented animal production.

Conference 29-30 Jun Technical tour 1 Jul Workshops 2-3 Jul

Conference

The main two-day program covers data collection and integration, emerging AI applications in precision livestock farming, farm and industry adoption, AI for science, datasets and benchmarks, digital biomarkers, and the ethical, environmental, and social implications of AI.

Technical tour

The ILVO technical tour gives participants a practical view of AI and precision livestock research in Flanders, including computer vision, audio, digital twinning, data sharing, emissions monitoring, robotics, renewable energy, and the value of data in animal nutrition.

Workshops

The week closes with two summer-school days: one on computer-vision applications in animal science and one on common pitfalls of machine learning in animal-science research.

Day 1Parallel scientific sessions

Data, PLF applications, adoption, AI for science, posters, and networking.

Day 2Plenary and specialist sessions

Benchmarks, digital biomarkers, ethical questions, closing, and awards.

Day 3ILVO technical tour

Demonstrations across AI, data infrastructure, animal monitoring, and agri-food technology.

Days 4-5Summer school

Hands-on learning around computer vision and robust machine learning practice.
