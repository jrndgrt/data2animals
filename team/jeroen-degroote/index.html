Jeroen Degroote is assistant professor at Ghent University, where he works within the Laboratory for Animal Nutrition and Animal Product Quality (LANUPRO), Department of Animal Sciences and Aquatic Ecology, Faculty of Bioscience Engineering.

His research focuses on animal nutrition for pigs and poultry, with attention to digestive physiology, feed evaluation, animal health, and data-driven monitoring. The work combines controlled experiments, lab analysis, and practical questions from animal nutrition.

The group increasingly connects experimental animal nutrition with monitoring systems such as electronic feeders, weighing devices, wearable sensors, and computer vision. These measurements are used together with mechanistic and data-driven models to improve nutrient use, animal health, and welfare.

Jeroen obtained a PhD in Bioscience Engineering in 2019 with work on the digestive physiology of weaned piglets. He teaches in pig and poultry nutrition, animal production systems, and precision livestock farming.
