NutriTwin

A digital-twin project for data-driven feeding strategies that support more sustainable and profitable pig production.

NutriTwin develops data-driven feeding strategies for more sustainable and profitable pig production. The project connects animal nutrition, farm data, chain data, and dynamic life-cycle assessment in a self-learning precision-feeding model.

Digital twin for pig production

From measurements to feeding scenarios

The central idea is to build a digital representation of nutrient flows on pig farms. By continuously updating the model with farm-specific data, the system can evolve from a generic digital twin into farm-specific versions that reflect changing animal needs, feed efficiency, and production context.

Decision support

Support practical feeding strategies for farms, feed companies, and the wider chain.

The project also addresses data interoperability and privacy. One direction is federated learning, where models can learn from distributed farm data without simply pooling raw data. This is important for practical adoption because the value of the tool depends on good data while farms and companies still need control over their information.

LANUPRO leads the project from Ghent University, together with partners including UGent Biovism Lab and ILVO groups in technology and animal sciences. Flanders’ FOOD manages the project.
