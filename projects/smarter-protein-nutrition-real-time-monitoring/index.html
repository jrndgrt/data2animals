Smarter protein nutrition through real-time monitoring

An Adisseo Research Grant project developing real-time monitoring for protein efficiency in weaned piglets.

This project was selected as one of the three laureates of the Adisseo Research Grant 2025, a global research initiative focused on swine nutrition.

Adisseo Research Grant 2025

Protein nutrition monitored animal by animal

The project focuses on developing a real-time monitoring system for protein efficiency in weaned piglets. By tracking individual animal responses, the system aims to support more precise protein supply, reduce overfeeding, improve feed conversion, and lower nitrogen emissions.

Weaned pigletsProduction phase Protein efficiencyMain response NitrogenEnvironmental target

The Ghent University team is led by Prof. Jeroen Degroote, with consortium partners at Agroscope in Switzerland. The project connects precision nutrition, animal monitoring, and environmental sustainability in the post-weaning phase.

Individual monitoring

Follow how each piglet responds to feed and environment.

Protein efficiency signal

Translate measurements into useful information about protein use.

Precision nutrition

Adjust protein supply more closely to animal needs.

Lower losses

Reduce avoidable overfeeding, feed waste, and nitrogen emissions.
