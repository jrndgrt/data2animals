Ex vivo gut models

In vitro and ex vivo gut models for digestion, permeability, mucosal response, antimicrobial activity, and microbial fermentation studies.

Ex vivo and in vitro gut models allow focused testing before, beside, or after animal experiments. They are useful when a protocol needs mechanistic information on digestion, mucosal function, transport, or microbial activity.

Available technologies include in vitro batch incubations simulating saliva, stomach, duodenum, jejunum, ileum, caecum, and colon conditions, Ussing chambers, intestinal tissue explant models, and everted sac models.

Readouts can include paracellular and transcellular permeability, mucosal absorption and excretion, transepithelial electrical resistance, short-circuit current, cytokine and chemokine production, gene expression, immunohistochemistry, nutrient digestibility, product stability, antimicrobial activity, and the effects of pH, enzymatic digestion, mucosal digestion, and microbial fermentation.
