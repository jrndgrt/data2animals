R&D service

Feed additive screening and development

Screening and development of antimicrobial, antioxidant, nutritional, and gut-health oriented feed additive concepts.

The group combines in vitro, ex vivo, and in vivo technologies to support development and evaluation of feed additive concepts.

Historically, the group contributed to antimicrobial feed additive research after the EU ban on antibiotic growth promoters in 2006, including early work on the beneficial effects of medium-chain fatty acids. Current work includes new applications and formulations of medium-chain fatty acids and antimicrobial essential oils such as thymol and cinnamaldehyde.

The platform also supports studies on oxidative stress, metabolic disorders, tissue and product quality, newborn and weaned piglet physiology, heat stress in finishing broilers, and glutathione metabolism.
